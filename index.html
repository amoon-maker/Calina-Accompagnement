What this accompaniment makes possible

  • Express what feels heavy or confusing.
  • Listen deeply to what is happening within you.
  • Understand your experience more clearly.
  • Regain greater clarity and inner safety.
  • Facilitate self-acceptance and access the inner qualities already present in you.
  • At your own pace.

This accompaniment may be for you if...

  • You feel your emotions deeply, but you do not know what to do with them. You often feel like you are carrying this alone.
  • There may be things you cannot truly express, even with people close to you.
  • You are going through a period of confusion, emotional fatigue, or transition.
  • You feel the need for a safe space to set down what you are living through and regain more calm, clarity, and inner safety.
  • Your faith is important to you, and you want your emotional experience to coexist in harmony with it.

The approach

  • A trauma-informed approach.
  • Rooted in somatic presence.
  • Based on Nonviolent Communication as taught by Marshall Rosenberg.
  • Inspired by the principles of Internal Family Systems, polyvagal theory, and attachment theory.

What this space is not

This is not a place to analyze your story or to follow the steps of a method.

Past experiences can be mentioned, but they are not the center.

It is a space to meet what is happening within you, with presence and listening.

What this space is

This space offers moments to pause.

Moments to bring presence where there is often tension, pressure, or urgency.

A place to let what is here exist: emotions, questions, and needs.

Without forcing anything.

With listening.

Moving gently, at the pace of what is alive.
